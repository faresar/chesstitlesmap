How to get chess title

Chess players can obtain various titles from the International Chess Federation (FIDE) through different methods. Here's a comprehensive overview of how to obtain each title:

Men chess titles

Grandmaster (GM)

  • Achieve a FIDE rating of 2500 or higher
  • Earn three GM norms in tournaments covering at least 27 games
  • Alternative paths:
    • Reach the final 16 in the FIDE World Cup
    • Win the World Junior Championship (U20)
    • Win the World Senior Championship (50+ or 65+ divisions)
    • Win a Continental championship

International Master (IM)

  • Achieve a FIDE rating of 2400 or higher
  • Earn three IM norms over 27 or more games
  • Alternative paths:
    • Qualify for the FIDE World Cup
    • Finish second or third in the World Junior Championship (U20)
    • Finish second or third in the World Senior Championship (50+ or 65+ divisions)
    • Win the World Youth Championship (U18)
    • Win the World Youth Championship (U16) outright
    • Finish second or third in a Continental championship
    • Win a Continental over-50, over-65, or under-20 championship
    • Win a sub-Continental championship

FIDE Master (FM)

  • Achieve a FIDE rating of 2300 or higher
  • No norms required

Candidate Master (CM)

  • Achieve a FIDE rating of 2200 or higher
  • No norms required
  • Alternative paths for players rated 2000-2199:
    • Finish in top three in World Youth Championship (U8 and U10)
    • Finish second or third in Continental U12, U14, or U16 championship
    • Score 50% or more over at least 7 games at an Olympiad

Women chess titles

Woman Grandmaster (WGM)

  • Achieve a FIDE rating of 2300 or higher
  • Earn three WGM norms in international competition

Woman International Master (WIM)

  • Achieve a FIDE rating of 2200 or higher
  • Earn three WIM norms in international competition

Woman FIDE Master (WFM)

  • Achieve a FIDE rating of 2100 or higher
  • No norms required

Woman Candidate Master (WCM)

  • Achieve a FIDE rating of 2000 or higher
  • No norms required

It's important to note that for GM, IM, WGM, and WIM titles, players must achieve both the required rating and norms. For FM, CM, WFM, and WCM titles, only the rating requirement needs to be met. Additionally, FIDE may award titles directly in certain high-level tournaments or championships, even if the usual requirements are not fully met.
