Men chess titles

GM
  • Achieve a FIDE rating of 2500 or higher
  • Earn three GM norms in tournaments covering at least 27 games
  • Alternative paths:
    • Reach the final 16 in the FIDE World Cup
    • Win the World Junior Championship (U20)
    • Win the World Senior Championship (50+ or 65+ divisions)
    • Win a Continental championship
IM
  • Achieve a FIDE rating of 2400 or higher
  • Earn three IM norms over 27 or more games
  • Alternative paths:
    • Qualify for the FIDE World Cup
    • Finish second/third in the World Junior Championship (U20)
    • Finish second/third in the World Senior Championship (50+ or 65+)
    • Win the World Youth Championship (U18 or U16)
    • Finish second/third in a Continental championship
    • Win a sub-Continental championship
FM
  • Achieve a FIDE rating of 2300 or higher
  • No norms required
CM
  • Achieve a FIDE rating of 2200 or higher
  • No norms required
  • Alternatives for players rated 2000–2199:
    • Finish in top three at the World Youth Championship (U8/U10)
    • Finish second/third in Continental U12, U14 or U16 championships
    • Score 50%+ over at least 7 games at an Olympiad

Women chess titles

WGM
  • Achieve a FIDE rating of 2300 or higher
  • Earn three WGM norms in international competition
WIM
  • Achieve a FIDE rating of 2200 or higher
  • Earn three WIM norms in international competition
WFM
  • Achieve a FIDE rating of 2100 or higher
  • No norms required
WCM
  • Achieve a FIDE rating of 2000 or higher
  • No norms required

It's important to note that for GM, IM, WGM, and WIM titles, players must achieve both the required rating and norms. For FM, CM, WFM, and WCM titles, only the rating requirement needs to be met. Additionally, FIDE may award titles directly in certain high-level tournaments or championships, even if the usual requirements are not fully met.
