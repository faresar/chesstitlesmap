Men chess titles

  • GM: Achieve a FIDE rating of 2500 or higher; earn three GM norms in tournaments covering at least 27 games. Alternative paths include reaching the final 16 in the FIDE World Cup, winning the World Junior Championship (U20), the World Senior Championship (50+ or 65+), or a Continental championship.
  • IM: Achieve a FIDE rating of 2400 or higher; earn three IM norms over 27 or more games. Alternative paths include qualifying for the FIDE World Cup, finishing second or third in the World Junior (U20) or World Senior Championships (50+/65+), winning the World Youth (U18/U16), a Continental championship, or a sub-Continental championship.
  • FM: Achieve a FIDE rating of 2300 or higher. No norms are required.
  • CM: Achieve a FIDE rating of 2200 or higher. No norms are required. For players rated between 2000 and 2199, alternatives include finishing in the top three at the World Youth Championship (U8/U10), securing a top-three finish in Continental U12, U14 or U16 championships, or scoring 50%+ over at least 7 games at an Olympiad.

Women chess titles

WGM
WIM
WFM
WCM
  • WGM: Achieve a FIDE rating of 2300 or higher; earn three WGM norms in international competition.
  • WIM: Achieve a FIDE rating of 2200 or higher; earn three WIM norms in international competition.
  • WFM: Achieve a FIDE rating of 2100 or higher. No norms are required.
  • WCM: Achieve a FIDE rating of 2000 or higher. No norms are required.

It's important to note that for GM, IM, WGM, and WIM titles, players must achieve both the required rating and norms. For FM, CM, WFM, and WCM titles, only the rating requirement needs to be met. Additionally, FIDE may award titles directly in certain high-level tournaments or championships, even if the usual requirements are not fully met.
